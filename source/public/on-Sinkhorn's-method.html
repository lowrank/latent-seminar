    on Sinkhorn's method

    Oct 02, 20222 min read

    Introduction

    Sinkhorn’s Theorem bridges the positive matrices (positive entries) with the doubly stochastic matrices by diagonal multipliers, i.e., there are diagonal matrices D1​ and D2​ that

    D1​AD2​e=e,eTD1​AD2​=eT

    The proof can be found in various methods, but most are based on certain kind of fixed-point property. For instance, the geometric proof uses the Brouwer’s fixed-point theorem. The convergence of the Sinkhorn’s algorithm seeks for the vectors x and y by iteratively computing

    yk+1​=r./(Axk​),xk+1​=c./(ATyk+1​)

    where r is the row sum vector and c is the column sum vector. They can be merged into one iteration by xk+1​=T(xk​):=c./(AT(r./Axk​)). The theory uses the nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theorem to find the fixed-point. Clearly, the map T sends the positive cone R+n​ to itself, here we need a little bit more compactness to exploit the fixed-point theorem. Either seeking for the boundedness or define Tx=T(x)/∥T(x)∥1​, then a fixed-point of T also works due to invariance of scaling. When the matrix A is relaxed to only nonnegative entries, there are proofs showing if A is fully indecomposable, then the same conclusion holds.

    By a simple generalization, it is natural to ask for non-singular integral kernel A(x,y)>0 such that

    ∫Dq​​p(x)A(x,y)q(y)dy=1,∫Dp​​p(x)A(x,y)q(y)dx=1

    Then a natural iteration will be hk+1​(y)=Thk​:=∫Dq​​∫Dp​​A(x,y)hk​(y)dyA(x,y)r(x)​dxc(y)​

    and we seek for a fixed-point of h=Th, where T is a normalization of T, the boundedness of hk​ comes at no price, for a certain kind of compactness, we can add the equi-continuity requirement which becomes a smoothness condition on A(x,y) such that A(x,y) is equi-continuous in y, then a convergent subsequence of hk​ will be what we need. Therefore A can be also a weakly singular kernel as well.

    If A(x,y,t) is an evolution of integral operator converges as t→∞ which gradually looses the equi-continuity or boundedness, it is interesting to see whether the corresponding solution h∗(y,t) also converges.
